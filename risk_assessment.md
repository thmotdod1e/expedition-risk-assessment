# Expedition Risk Assessment

## Hazards

### 1. Avalanche Risk
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Impact:** High
- **Mitigation:** Avoid slopes >30 degrees after fresh snowfall.

### 2. Crevasse Falls
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Impact:** High
- **Mitigation:** Rope team travel, crevasse rescue training.

### 3. Altitude Sickness
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Impact:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Gradual ascent, acclimatization days.

### 4. Extreme Weather
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Impact:** High
- **Mitigation:** Monitor forecasts, carry emergency shelter.

### 5. Serac Fall Potential
- **Likelihood:** Low
- **Impact:** Catastrophic
- **Mitigation:** Avoid serac zones, travel at night when stable.

**New Icefall Hazard Identified:** Recent satellite imagery indicates increased calving activity in the eastern icefall. The serac instability may pose additional risk during midday temperature peaks. Team must consider alternate route via western ridge.

## Equipment Checklist
- Personal anchor tethers (rated 22 kN)
- 60-meter 7mm Perlon cord (2x)
- Ice screws, snow pickets
- Satellite communicator
- First aid kit
